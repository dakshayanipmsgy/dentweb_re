4. Operation & Maintenance Guidelines

Operation and Maintenance Guidelines

To ensure optimal performance and longevity of your solar system, it is essential to follow proper operation and maintenance practices. This section provides clear and practical instructions tailored for your system type.

1. Operation Guidelines

Solar Panels

  • Ensure that the panels are exposed to direct sunlight without any obstructions such as trees, buildings, or dust.
  • Monitor panel surfaces regularly to identify dirt, bird droppings, or debris that may reduce efficiency.

Inverter

  • Always keep the inverter in a well-ventilated and shaded area to prevent overheating.
  • Check the inverter display periodically to ensure it is functioning normally. Look out for error messages or warning lights.
  • For hybrid or off-grid systems, ensure the battery charge status is regularly checked on the inverter.

Battery Backup (if applicable)

  • Avoid completely discharging the battery frequently, as it may impact its lifespan.
  • Use appliances judiciously during non-solar hours to preserve battery life.
  • Monitor battery health using the inverter or a dedicated monitoring system.

2. Maintenance Schedule

Task Frequency Description
Solar Panel Cleaning Every 2–3 months Use water and a soft cloth or sponge to clean the panels. Avoid abrasive materials or harsh chemicals.
Inverter Inspection Monthly Check for error codes, proper ventilation, and dust accumulation.
Battery Check (if hybrid or off-grid) Monthly Monitor charge-discharge cycles and ensure proper storage conditions.
Wiring Inspection Biannually Look for wear, tear, or rodent damage. Ensure all connections are secure.
System Performance Review Annually Verify energy generation and overall system health using monitoring tools or reports from service visits.

3. Cleaning Instructions

Solar Panels

  • Use clean water and a non-abrasive cloth or sponge to gently remove dust and debris.
  • Avoid cleaning during hot midday hours to prevent thermal shock (cold water on hot panels).
  • If panels are in a hard-to-reach area, consider hiring professional cleaners.

Inverter

  • Dust the inverter unit regularly to ensure adequate airflow.
  • Never open the inverter casing; leave technical inspections and repairs to certified technicians.

Battery Backup

  • Keep the battery in a dry, cool location away from direct sunlight.
  • Check for any signs of swelling, leakage, or unusual behaviour.

4. Troubleshooting Guide – Common Issues and Solutions

Issue Possible Cause Recommended Action
Low Energy Generation Dirt on panels, shading issues, or cloudy weather. Clean panels and check for obstructions.
Inverter Error Code or Alarm Grid instability, wiring issues, or inverter malfunction. Reset the inverter. If the issue persists, contact support.
Battery Drains Quickly (Hybrid/Off-Grid) Excessive usage during non-solar hours or battery aging. Reduce load or contact support for a battery check.
System Shutdown Tripped circuit breaker or wiring issue. Check circuit breakers and reconnect if necessary. Contact support if unresolved.

5. Monitoring and Optimization

For On-Grid Systems

  • Regularly review net metering statements to track energy credits or savings.
  • Ensure grid availability to maximize system performance.

For Hybrid Systems

  • Monitor energy flow between solar panels, the grid, and the battery using the inverter display or mobile app.
  • Schedule heavy appliance usage (e.g., washing machines, water heaters) during peak sunlight hours.

For Off-Grid Systems

  • Use the system wisely to avoid depleting the battery reserve.
  • Plan your energy usage according to weather conditions and battery storage capacity.

7. Consumer Education & Best Practices

Consumer Education and Best Practices

To maximize the efficiency and lifespan of your solar system, it is essential to adopt certain best practices and gain a basic understanding of system maintenance and operations.

1. Best Practices for System Maintenance

Solar Panel Care

Regular Cleaning:

  • Clean panels every 2–3 months or more frequently in dusty or polluted environments.
  • Use plain water and a soft cloth or sponge to remove dirt and bird droppings.
  • Avoid abrasive materials and harsh chemicals.
  • Clean panels in the morning or late evening to avoid thermal shock.

Shading and Obstructions:

  • Ensure there are no shadows from trees, buildings, or other objects on the panels.
  • Trim nearby vegetation regularly to maintain maximum sunlight exposure.

Inverter Maintenance

Placement:

  • Keep the inverter in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or water.

Error Monitoring:

  • Check the inverter display regularly for error codes or warning lights.
  • Report any irregularities immediately via email or WhatsApp for prompt attention.

Battery Care (if applicable)

Regular Checks:

  • Monitor charge-discharge cycles using the inverter or monitoring app.
  • Avoid complete discharge or overcharging to maintain battery health.

Storage Conditions:

  • Keep the battery in a cool, dry location, away from direct sunlight and moisture.
  • Inspect for physical signs of damage, swelling, or leaks.

2. Monitoring System Performance

Daily Monitoring

  • Check your system’s daily energy generation on the inverter or app.
  • Look for consistent output based on sunlight conditions.

Monthly Review

  • Compare monthly generation with expected performance values.
  • Variations may indicate shading, dirt accumulation, or technical issues.

Annual Performance Review

  • Utilize performance reports provided by Dakshayani Enterprises (under AMC/CMC) for detailed analysis.
